Generate two sets of short English texts, 30 texts in each set.
Each text must consist of exactly 4 short, simple sentences
(roughly 10 to 15 words each), and the last sentence must
end with a period.

Set A ("causal"): Each text should be a short narrative describing
a sequence of events in temporal/causal order. The 4 sentences
should describe things that happen one after another.

Example (use this as the first entry of causal_texts):
"The glass slipped from her hand. It fell to the floor. It broke into many pieces. She swept them up carefully."

Set B ("non-causal"): Each text should consist of 4 independent
factual statements on a common theme. The statements should have
no temporal, causal, or discourse dependency on each other.
In particular, avoid anaphoric references between sentences (e.g.,
"also", "the above", "all three") and avoid listing items in a
conventional ascending or descending order (e.g., by size, date,
or number).

Example (use this as the first entry of noncausal_texts):
"A violin is played with a bow. A flute is played by blowing air. A drum is played by striking it. A harp is played by plucking strings."

Output the result as a JSON object with the following structure:
{
  "metadata": {
    "model": "<your model name and version>",
    "date": "<today's date and year>",
    "note": "Output used as-is without manual revision or selection."
  },
  "causal_texts": [<list of 30 strings>],
  "noncausal_texts": [<list of 30 strings>]
}
Output only the JSON with no other text.
